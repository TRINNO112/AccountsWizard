The Origin Story Nobody Asked For

Seriously, nobody asked. But here we are anyway.

AccountsWizard basically started as mera ek chhota sa dream: accounting seekhna thoda easy aur visual bana du.
Matlab ki graphs, infographics, aur visuals ke saath concepts ko samajhna thoda simple ho jaye.

Ab yeh website perfect hai? Nahi yaar. Bugs hain, kuch features incomplete hain, aur shayad sabko yeh “fun” na lage. Par idea tha ki jo bhi bana sakun, best try karun aur seekhne wale ke liye thoda easy aur interactive banaun.

Humor bhi add kiya hai, kyunki thodi hasi-masti ke bina accounting ki duniya thodi boring ho jati hai. Toh expect mat karo ki yeh world-class entertainment hai, par haan, thodi smile zaroor milegi. 😅

Is website ka main maksad? Sirf ek simple aur visual way me accounts ko present karna, taaki students concepts ko thoda easily samajh sake. Agar sapna pura nahi hua, koi baat nahi, phir bhi main continuously try karunga ki yeh aur better ho jaye.
Short me: dream, effort, thodi masti, aur kaafi bugs. Lekin pura dil laga ke banaya hai.

My Chaotic Journey with AccountsWizard

A timeline full of trials, errors, personal lessons, and a little humor

August 2025
I started this project with high hopes and zero experience in structured web development. Dabbled in Node.js, npm, and tried component-based code. Hours of trial and error later, I had written thousands of lines of code, but only a few things actually displayed correctly. It was chaotic, frustrating, but every small fix felt like a huge personal victory. 😅
September 2025
After struggling with component-based code, I switched back to static HTML. It felt like a simplification, but a necessary one. Bugs still lurked everywhere, random errors made me question my sanity, yet small progress kept motivating me to continue.
Exams Time
During exams, I got very poor grades, especially in Economics. Honestly, it was disheartening. But Nirmit Sir constantly supported me, reminded me that learning is a journey, and encouraged me to keep working on AccountsWizard. Without that support, I honestly might have quit. His guidance became the reason I continued this project despite setbacks.
October 2025
With static HTML, I slowly rebuilt the website. I experimented with infographics, visuals, and interactive elements to make concepts easier to understand. Bugs and errors were still present, but seeing small pieces finally work gave me real motivation. Every little success felt huge.
Present
AccountsWizard is now functional, albeit imperfect. The journey taught me patience, persistence, and the importance of support from mentors. This project is my experiment, my personal learning, and a little bit of humor mixed in — just like my coding life. 😄

My Experiment (If You Can Call It That)

Spoiler: Mostly about testing my own skills and wasting a lot of time

Why I Did This

Look, I could write something fancy about "revolutionizing accounting education," but let’s be honest — I’m still a student and this is just my personal experiment. I wanted to see how much I can cover, how interactive a page can get, and how visuals can help explain concepts.

I developed this project entirely by myself, line by line, sometimes struggling, sometimes learning. Neon colors were mostly an experiment on the About page — the main pages are simpler, more functional. Think of it as me trying out crazy ideas without hurting anyone’s eyes… hopefully. 😅

The goal wasn’t to teach anyone officially — it was about exploring, experimenting, learning coding, layouts, interactivity, and testing my limits. And yes, maybe adding a bit of humor here and there, because why not?

Frequently Avoided Questions

Questions nobody asked, but I’m answering anyway

Is this a real accounting course?

+
Define "real." This isn’t a certified course. Every chapter is inspired by the DK Goel syllabus, so you’ll find actual accounting principles here, but it’s mostly an experimental platform created by me to learn, test, and explore coding & accounting together. Think of it as a guided chaos experience, not a formal class.

Why aren’t there neon colors everywhere?

+
Neon colors were mostly a testing experiment on the About page. Main pages are simpler, more readable, and focused on usability. Your eyes can relax… for now. 😎

How did you organize the chapters and quizzes?

+
Each chapter follows the DK Goel syllabus, section by section. I designed quizzes (MCQs, true/false, scenario-based) to test understanding. AI helped in generating code and ideas, but the flow, structure, and questions were carefully planned by me.

Will this website make me an accounting expert?

+
Nope. It will help you understand concepts better and see how interactive tools can enhance learning. You’ll also witness how a student experiments, deals with errors, and builds a project with a mix of AI, Google, and reference material.

Did you face a lot of problems while building it?

+
Absolutely. Animations breaking, Firebase integration issues, quiz functions not triggering — I lost days figuring it all out. AI never gets everything right the first time, so I had to explain multiple times, debug, and sometimes copy directly from DK Goel to ensure correctness. Every problem was a lesson in patience and persistence. 😅

Who should really take this website seriously?

+
Students who want to explore accounting with an experimental approach, see how interactive learning works, or just watch one student struggle and learn. If you expect perfection, you might be disappointed — but if you want creativity, mistakes, humor, and learning, this is for you.
