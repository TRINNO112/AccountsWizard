üë§ 1. Sole Proprietorship (Ekaki Vyapaar)

üìñ Definition

Sole Proprietorship wo business form hai jisme ek hi vyakti business ka owner hota hai. Wo akela hi saari decisions leta hai, saara profit apne paas rakhta hai, aur saare losses bhi akela hi bear karta hai. Ye sabse purana aur simple form hai business ka.

üîπ Single Ownership
Business ka ek hi malik hota hai jo saara investment karta hai
üîπ No Separate Legal Entity
Owner aur business alag nahi hote - dono ek hi maane jaate hain
üîπ Unlimited Liability
Agar business mein loss ho toh owner ki personal property bhi use ho sakti hai debts pay karne ke liye
üîπ No Legal Formalities
Koi special registration ya legal procedure nahi chahiye (except specific trades)

‚úÖ Advantages (Fayde)

  • Easy formation - Bahut aasaan start karna
  • Full control - Saare decisions khud le sakte ho
  • All profits - Poora profit khud rakh sakte ho
  • Secrecy - Business secrets safe
  • Quick decisions - Kisi se puchne ki zaroorat nahi
  • Direct motivation - Mehnat ka poora result khud ko milta hai
  • Personal touch - Customers se direct relation
  • Flexibility - Easily change kar sakte ho business

‚ùå Disadvantages (Nuksan)

  • Unlimited liability - Personal property risk mein
  • Limited capital - Sirf apne paise se hi kaam
  • Limited skills - Ek insaan sab kuch nahi jaanta
  • Uncertain life - Owner ke saath business bhi khatam
  • No economies of scale - Large scale benefits nahi
  • Overburden - Saara kaam akele karna
  • Limited growth - Expansion difficult hai

üìç Examples of Sole Proprietorship:

‚Ä¢ Retail Shops: Kirana store, Medical store, Stationery shop
‚Ä¢ Service Providers: Tailors, Beauticians, Tutors
‚Ä¢ Professionals: Doctors, Lawyers, CAs (practicing individually)
‚Ä¢ Small Manufacturers: Cottage industries, Handicrafts
‚Ä¢ Food Business: Small restaurants, Food stalls

‚ö†Ô∏è Suitability (Kab suitable hai?):

‚úì Jab capital requirement kam ho
‚úì Jab personal attention zaroori ho
‚úì Jab quick decisions lene ho
‚úì Small scale operations ke liye
‚úì Jab secrecy important ho

üè† 3. Hindu Undivided Family (HUF) Business

üìñ Definition

HUF Business wo business hai jo Hindu family ke ancestral property se chalta hai. Ye sirf Hindu, Jain, Sikh aur Buddhist families ke liye applicable hai.

üîπ Karta
Family ka sabse senior male member jo business manage karta hai. Uski liability unlimited hoti hai.
üîπ Coparceners
Karta + uske sons, grandsons, great-grandsons. Unki liability limited hoti hai (ancestral property tak).
üîπ Ancestral Property
Wo property jo 4 generations se family mein chal rahi hai

‚úÖ Features of HUF Business

  • Governed by Hindu Law
  • Membership by birth
  • Karta has full control
  • Minor can also be member
  • Three generations can be coparceners
  • Female members are now coparceners (after 2005 amendment)

üìã Rights of Coparceners

  • Right to joint possession
  • Right to partition
  • Right to accounts
  • Right to restrain Karta from misusing property
  • Right to maintenance

üè™ 5. Cooperative Society

üìñ Definition

Cooperative Society ek voluntary association hai jisme log apni common economic needs ko fulfill karne ke liye milkar kaam karte hain. Ye "all for each and each for all" principle par kaam karti hai.

üåü Core Principles of Cooperation

1. Voluntary Membership

Koi bhi join kar sakta hai, koi bhi chhod sakta hai

2. Democratic Control

One member = One vote, chahe kitna bhi share ho

3. Limited Return on Capital

Dividend limited hota hai (usually 9-12%)

4. Distribution of Surplus

Profits members mein patronage basis par divide

Types of Cooperative Societies:

üõí Consumer Cooperative

  • Consumers ke liye goods buy karna
  • Middlemen eliminate karna
  • Fair prices provide karna
  • Example: Kendriya Bhandar, Apna Bazar

üè≠ Producer Cooperative

  • Small producers ka association
  • Raw materials together buy karna
  • Collectively produce karna
  • Example: Handloom cooperatives

üí∞ Credit Cooperative

  • Members ko loans dena
  • Savings encourage karna
  • Low interest rates
  • Example: Village credit societies

üè† Housing Cooperative

  • Members ke liye houses banana
  • Land acquire karna collectively
  • Construction karwana
  • Example: DDA housing

üåæ Marketing Cooperative

  • Farmers ka produce sell karna
  • Fair prices dilwana
  • Example: AMUL, Mother Dairy

üöú Farming Cooperative

  • Small farmers ka land pool karna
  • Joint cultivation
  • Sharing of equipment

‚úÖ Advantages of Cooperative Society

  • Easy formation - min 10 members
  • Limited liability
  • Democratic management
  • Stable existence
  • Government support
  • Tax benefits
  • Service motive (not profit)

‚ùå Disadvantages of Cooperative Society

  • Limited capital
  • Inefficient management
  • Lack of secrecy
  • Government interference
  • Conflicts among members
  • Lack of motivation
  • Corruption possible

üìä Registration Requirements:

‚Ä¢ Minimum 10 members required for registration
‚Ä¢ Registered under Cooperative Societies Act, 1912 or State Acts
‚Ä¢ Registrar of Cooperative Societies ke paas register hoti hai

üìñ Definition

Insurance ek contract hai jisme insurer (company) agree karti hai ki wo insured (customer) ko specific loss hone par compensation degi, iske badle mein customer premium pay karta hai.

Types of Insurance

üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß Life Insurance

Principles of Insurance:

1. Utmost Good Faith (Uberrimae Fidei)
Dono parties ko poora sach batana chahiye. Koi bhi material fact chhupana nahi chahiye.
2. Insurable Interest
Insured ko subject matter mein financial interest hona chahiye. Loss se directly affect hona chahiye.
3. Indemnity
Insurance sirf actual loss ka compensation deta hai, profit nahi. Insured original position mein wapas aana chahiye.
4. Subrogation
Claim pay karne ke baad, insurer ko insured ki jagah lene ka right milta hai against third party.
5. Contribution
Agar same subject ke liye multiple insurance ho, toh sab insurers milkar proportionately pay karenge.
6. Proximate Cause (Causa Proxima)
Claim tab milega jab loss ka immediate/nearest cause insured peril ho.
7. Mitigation of Loss
Insured ko loss minimize karne ki koshish karni chahiye.

‚ö†Ô∏è Life Insurance vs General Insurance

Life Insurance: Person ki life insure hoti hai. Indemnity principle apply nahi hota (fixed sum assured milta hai).

General Insurance: Property/assets insure hote hain. Indemnity principle apply hota hai (actual loss ka compensation).

üå± Chapter 6: Social Responsibility of Business & Business Ethics

üìå Introduction

Business ek social organ hai. Ye society se resources (men, money, material) leta hai, isliye society ko wapas kuch dena business ki duty banti hai. Profit kamana zaroori hai, lekin sirf profit hi maqsad nahi ho sakta.

üèóÔ∏è 1. Kinds of Social Responsibility

Social responsibility sirf ek tarah ki nahi hoti. Isko 4 categories mein divide kiya gaya hai:

1. Economic Responsibility
Sabse basic responsibility. Goods produce karna aur profit kamana taki business survive kare aur grow kare.
2. Legal Responsibility
Desh ke laws ko follow karna. Law-abiding citizen ki tarah business karna. (e.g., Taxes pay karna).
3. Ethical Responsibility
Wo kaam karna jo society expect karti hai, bhale hi law mein likha na ho. Right aur Fair behave karna.
4. Discretionary (Philanthropic) Responsibility
Apni marzi se society ka bhala karna without any obligation. (e.g., Donation to charity, building schools).

ü§ù 2. Arguments for & Against CSR

‚úÖ Arguments FOR (Kyun karna chahiye?)

  • Justification for Existence: Society ke bina business nahi chal sakta.
  • Long-term Interest: Achhi image se long run mein profit badhta hai.
  • Avoidance of Gov Regulation: Agar business khud acha karega, toh Gov strict laws nahi layegi.
  • Maintenance of Society: Crime aur poverty kam hogi toh business ka environment safe rahega.
  • Availability of Resources: Business ke paas money aur talent hai social problems solve karne ke liye.

‚ùå Arguments AGAINST (Kyun nahi?)

  • Profit Maximization: Main goal profit hai, social work nahi.
  • Burden on Consumers: CSR ka kharcha ghum-phir ke products mehange karke consumers se hi liya jayega.
  • Lack of Social Skills: Businessmen business chala sakte hain, social problems solve karna unka expertise nahi hai.
  • Lack of Public Support: Public hamesha business ke social involvement ko pasand nahi karti.

üë• 3. Responsibility Towards Different Groups

Business ki zimmedari alag-alag logon ke liye alag hoti hai (Stakeholders):

üí∞ Shareholders/Owners

  • Fair Return on Investment (ROI).
  • Assets ki safety ensure karna.
  • Accurate financial reports dena.
  • Company ki prestige badhana.

üë®‚Äçüíº Employees/Workers

  • Fair wages aur salary time pe dena.
  • Safe working environment.
  • Growth aur promotion ke chances.
  • Trade unions banane ka rights respect karna.

üõí Consumers

  • Right quantity aur quality products.
  • Fair prices (MRP se zyada nahi).
  • Adulteration (milawat) nahi karna.
  • Honest advertising.
  • Consumer grievances (shikayat) sunna.

üèõÔ∏è Government

  • Taxes honesty se pay karna.
  • Rules aur laws follow karna.
  • Corruption aur bribery se door rehna.
  • National problems mein help karna.

üåç Community/Society

  • Environment ko protect karna.
  • Jobs create karna (Employment).
  • Local amenities (parks, schools) mein help karna.
  • Weaker sections ki madad karna.

üå≥ 4. Environmental Protection

Environment ko bachana business ke liye critical hai kyunki pollution se health aur quality of life kharab hoti hai.

Types of Pollution:

  • Air Pollution: Factories ka dhuan (smoke), chemicals. Causes ozone depletion, global warming.
  • Water Pollution: Industrial waste nadiyon (rivers) mein daalna. Kills aquatic life.
  • Land Pollution: Garbage aur toxic waste zameen par dump karna. Makes soil infertile.
  • Noise Pollution: Heavy machinery aur generators ka shor. Causes hearing loss, stress.

Need for Pollution Control

1. Reduce Health Hazards: Cancer, heart/lung diseases kam karne ke liye.
2. Reduced Liability Risk: Pollution se hone wale nuksan ka compensation dene se bachne ke liye.
3. Cost Saving: Waste treatment aur recycling se cost bachti hai.
4. Public Image: Green companies ki respect zyada hoti hai.

Role of Business in Environment Protection

  • Top management ka commitment hona chahiye environment ke liye.
  • Clear policies banana aur employees ko communicate karna.
  • Eco-friendly techniques aur raw material use karna.
  • Laws follow karna aur Govt programs mein participate karna.
  • Pollution control workshops organize karna.

‚öñÔ∏è 5. Business Ethics

üìñ Concept

'Ethics' word Greek word 'Ethos' se aaya hai jiska matlab hai character. Business Ethics ka matlab hai business standards jo society ke view mein 'Right' ya 'Wrong' hain.

Elements of Business Ethics

  • Top Management Commitment: CEO aur managers ko role model banna padega.
  • Publication of Code: Written document ('Code of Conduct') hona chahiye jo bataye kya karna hai, kya nahi.
  • Establishment of Compliance Mechanisms: Check karne ka system ki ethics follow ho rahe hain ya nahi.
  • Involving Employees at all levels: Sirf bosses nahi, workers bhi ethics follow karein.
  • Measuring Results: Audit karna ki kitna ethical kaam hua.

üí∞ Chapter 7: Sources of Business Finance

üìå Introduction

Jaise human body ko blood chahiye, waise hi business ko Finance chahiye. Business Finance ka matlab hai wo paisa jo business activities (production, distribution) ke liye chahiye.

Need for Finance

  • Fixed Capital Requirement: Land, Building, Machinery kharidne ke liye (Long term).
  • Working Capital Requirement: Daily expenses jaise wages, raw material, bills (Short term).
  • Growth & Expansion: Business bada karne ke liye.

üìä 1. Classification of Sources

1. On basis of Period (Time)

  • Long Term (> 5 years): Equity Shares, Debentures, Retained Earnings, Loans from Financial Institutions.
  • Medium Term (1-5 years): Public Deposits, Lease Financing, Commercial Banks loans.
  • Short Term (< 1 year): Trade Credit, Bank Overdraft, Commercial Paper.

2. On basis of Ownership

  • Owner's Funds: Equity Shares, Preference Shares, Retained Earnings. (Permanent capital, No security required).
  • Borrowed Funds: Debentures, Loans, Public Deposits. (Fixed period, Interest pay karna padta hai, Security often required).

3. On basis of Source

  • Internal Sources: Generated within business (Retained Earnings).
  • External Sources: Outside se paisa lena (Shares, Debentures, Loans).

üè† 2. Owner's Funds (Equity Capital)

üìà A. Equity Shares

Ye company ke real owners hote hain. Inka paisa company ki life khatam hone par hi wapas milta hai.

  • Merits: Permanent capital, no fixed burden of dividend, voting rights control.
  • Demerits: Risk of fluctuating return, issue process complicated/expensive, dilution of control.
‚≠ê B. Preference Shares

Inhe 2 cheezon mein 'Preference' (priority) milti hai: (1) Dividend pehle milta hai, (2) Company band hone par capital pehle wapas milti hai.

Types: Cumulative (juda hua dividend), Non-cumulative, Participating (extra profit mein hissa), Convertible (equity mein convert hone wale).

üíµ C. Retained Earnings (Ploughing Back of Profits)

Profit ka wo hissa jo shareholders ko nahi diya gaya balki business mein wapas laga diya.

  • Merits: Cheapest source (koi interest nahi), increases financial strength.
  • Demerits: Dissatisfaction among shareholders (kam dividend), uncertain source (profit nahi toh saving nahi).

üè¶ 3. Borrowed Funds

üìú Debentures

  • Company ka loan certificate.
  • Interest: Fixed rate pe dena hi padta hai (profit ho ya loss).
  • Tax Benefit: Interest paid is tax-deductible expense.
  • No Voting Rights: Control dilute nahi hota.
  • Risk: Company ke liye risky hai agar profit na ho.

üè¢ Public Deposits

  • Directly public se paisa lena (FD ki tarah).
  • Interest rate bank se zyada milti hai public ko, lekin bank loan se sasta padta hai company ko.
  • Unsecured hote hain.
  • Max period: 3 years.

üè¶ Loans from Financial Institutions (FIs) & Banks

  • Commercial Banks: Short/Medium term loans provided against security.
  • Financial Institutions (Development Banks): Like IFCI, SIDBI, LIC. Provide long term finance + managerial advice. Often called "Public Financial Institutions".

Equity vs Debentures (Difference):

Basis Equity Shares Debentures
Nature Own Capital Borrowed Capital (Loan)
Return Dividend (Not fixed) Interest (Fixed)
Voting Rights Yes (Full control) No
Risk High risk for investor Low risk for investor
Security Not secured Usually secured by assets

‚è±Ô∏è 4. Other Important Sources

üîπ Trade Credit
Supplier se udhaar maal kharidna. Ye short-term finance ka sabse common source hai. No cash exchange immediately.
üîπ Inter-Corporate Deposits (ICD)
Ek company doosri company ko short term loan deti hai. Unsecured. Very fast processing. High interest rate.
üîπ Commercial Paper (CP)
Large, credit-worthy companies issue karti hain. Unsecured promissory note. 15 days to 1 year.
üîπ Lease Financing
Asset ko kharidne ki bajaye rent pe lena. Investment bachta hai. Rent tax-deductible hai.

üåç 5. International Sources

Agar Indian company ko bahar (foreign) se paisa uthana hai:

GDR (Global Depository Receipt)

  • Issued in international markets (e.g., Europe).
  • Denominated in Dollars/Euros.
  • Traded on foreign stock exchanges.
  • Represents shares of Indian company.

ADR (American Depository Receipt)

  • Issued ONLY to citizens of USA.
  • Traded on US Stock exchanges (like NYSE, NASDAQ).
  • Stricter disclosure norms than GDR.

IDR (Indian Depository Receipt)

  • When FOREIGN companies want to raise funds from INDIA.
  • Listed on Indian stock exchanges.

ü§î 6. Factors Affecting Choice of Source

  • üí∞ Cost: Interest rate aur floatation cost (issue karne ka kharcha) kam hona chahiye.
  • üìâ Financial Strength: Agar company stable hai toh debt le sakti hai.
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è Risk: Debt risky hai (interest dena hi padega), Equity safe hai company ke liye.
  • üéÆ Control: Agar owners control share nahi karna chahte, toh Equity issue mat karo (Debt lo).
  • üìÖ Time Period: Short term ke liye Trade Credit/Bank loan, Long term ke liye Equity.
