Accounting is often called the ‚Äúlanguage of business.‚Äù It is a process of recording, classifying, summarising, and interpreting financial information so that decisions can be made effectively.

According to the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA), accounting is ‚Äúthe art of recording, classifying, and summarising in a significant manner and in terms of money, transactions and events which are, in part at least, of a financial character, and interpreting the results thereof.‚Äù

‚ö° Objectives of Accounting

  • To keep systematic records of financial transactions.
  • To determine profit or loss during an accounting period.
  • To ascertain financial position of the business (assets and liabilities).
  • To provide information for decision-making.
  • To safeguard business properties from misuse.

üìë Importance of Accounting

Accounting plays a crucial role in every business, small or large. It helps business owners, investors, creditors, and the government by providing relevant financial information.

  • Owners: know the profitability and financial position.
  • Investors: assess performance before investing further.
  • Government: ensures taxation and regulations are followed.
  • Creditors: check liquidity before giving loans or goods on credit.

‚ú® Features of Accounting

  • It deals with financial transactions only.
  • Transactions are recorded in monetary terms.
  • It follows systematic rules (Double Entry system).
  • It provides results in the form of Profit & Loss A/c and Balance Sheet.

üìù Short Notes (Revision)

üìå Definition & Concept

  • Accounting = Language of Business (AICPA Definition)
  • Process: Recording ‚Üí Classifying ‚Üí Summarizing ‚Üí Interpreting financial transactions
  • Only monetary transactions are recorded (Money Measurement Concept)
  • Business is separate from owner (Business Entity Concept)

üéØ Main Objectives

  • Maintain systematic record of all financial transactions
  • Calculate profit/loss for a specific period
  • Determine financial position (Assets, Liabilities, Capital)
  • Provide information for decision-making
  • Protect business assets through proper records

üë• Stakeholders & Their Needs

  • Owners: Track profitability and business growth
  • Investors: Evaluate investment opportunities
  • Government: Ensure tax compliance and regulations
  • Creditors: Assess creditworthiness
  • Managers: Make operational decisions
  • Employees: Understand company's stability

‚ö° Key Features

  • Monetary Terms: All transactions recorded in money
  • Systematic Process: Follows standard procedures
  • Double Entry System: Every debit has a credit
  • Evidence Based: Based on documents and vouchers
  • Period Specific: Reports for defined time periods
  • Universal Rules: Follows accepted principles (GAAP)

üìä Output & Reports

  • Trading Account: Shows gross profit/loss
  • Profit & Loss Account: Shows net profit/loss
  • Balance Sheet: Shows financial position
  • Cash Flow Statement: Shows cash movements
