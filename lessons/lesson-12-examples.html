How These Books Connect

In this example, we'll follow ABC Traders (a business) through multiple transactions in January 2025. You'll see the same parties appearing in different books, showing how subsidiary books work together as a complete system.

Key Understanding: When ABC Traders buys goods on credit, it goes to the Purchase Book. If they return some goods, it goes to the Purchase Returns Book. Both transactions involve the same supplier but are recorded in different books!

Complete Business Cycle Example

All Transactions for ABC Traders - January 2025:

Jan 3: Purchased goods from Raj Suppliers ₹50,000 on credit (Invoice P-101)

Jan 5: Sold goods to Kumar Enterprises ₹35,000 on credit (Invoice S-201)

Jan 8: Purchased goods from Sharma & Co. ₹28,000 on credit (Invoice P-102)

Jan 10: Returned defective goods to Raj Suppliers ₹5,000 (Debit Note DN-01)

Jan 12: Sold goods to Patel Traders ₹42,000 on credit (Invoice S-202)

Jan 15: Kumar Enterprises returned damaged goods ₹3,000 (Credit Note CN-01)

Jan 18: Purchased goods from Gupta Brothers ₹33,000 on credit (Invoice P-103)

Jan 20: Purchased furniture from Office Mart ₹25,000 on credit (needs Journal Proper)

Jan 25: Sold goods to Mehta Ltd. ₹38,500 on credit (Invoice S-203)

Jan 28: Returned excess stock to Sharma & Co. ₹2,500 (Debit Note DN-02)

Notice the Connections:

  • Raj Suppliers appears in both Purchase Book (Jan 3) and Purchase Returns Book (Jan 10)
  • Kumar Enterprises appears in both Sales Book (Jan 5) and Sales Returns Book (Jan 15)
  • Sharma & Co. appears in both Purchase Book (Jan 8) and Purchase Returns Book (Jan 28)
  • Furniture purchase goes to Journal Proper (not Purchase Book) because it's an asset!

1. Purchase Book

Remember: Only CREDIT purchases of GOODS for resale are recorded here.

Purchase Book of ABC Traders:

Date Particulars Invoice No. L.F. Amount (₹)
Jan 3, 2025 Raj Suppliers P-101 15 50,000
Jan 8, 2025 Sharma & Co. P-102 22 28,000
Jan 18, 2025 Gupta Brothers P-103 28 33,000
Total 1,11,000

Ledger Posting:

Purchases A/c Dr. ₹1,11,000

To Sundry Creditors A/c ₹1,11,000

(Being total credit purchases for January)

Individual Postings:

• Raj Suppliers A/c credited with ₹50,000

• Sharma & Co. A/c credited with ₹28,000

• Gupta Brothers A/c credited with ₹33,000

Why furniture purchase (Jan 20) is NOT here? Because furniture is an ASSET, not goods for resale. It goes to Journal Proper!

2. Sales Book

Remember: Only CREDIT sales of GOODS are recorded here.

Sales Book of ABC Traders:

Date Particulars Invoice No. L.F. Amount (₹)
Jan 5, 2025 Kumar Enterprises S-201 12 35,000
Jan 12, 2025 Patel Traders S-202 18 42,000
Jan 25, 2025 Mehta Ltd. S-203 25 38,500
Total 1,15,500

Ledger Posting:

Sundry Debtors A/c Dr. ₹1,15,500

To Sales A/c ₹1,15,500

(Being total credit sales for January)

Individual Postings:

• Kumar Enterprises A/c debited with ₹35,000

• Patel Traders A/c debited with ₹42,000

• Mehta Ltd. A/c debited with ₹38,500

3. Purchase Returns Book (Returns Outward)

Remember: Goods returned TO suppliers who sold us goods on credit.

Purchase Returns Book of ABC Traders:

Date Particulars Debit Note No. L.F. Amount (₹)
Jan 10, 2025 Raj Suppliers DN-01 15 5,000
Jan 28, 2025 Sharma & Co. DN-02 22 2,500
Total 7,500

Ledger Posting:

Sundry Creditors A/c Dr. ₹7,500

To Purchase Returns A/c ₹7,500

(Being goods returned to suppliers)

Individual Postings:

• Raj Suppliers A/c debited with ₹5,000

• Sharma & Co. A/c debited with ₹2,500

See the Connection:

Raj Suppliers: We bought ₹50,000 (Purchase Book) but returned ₹5,000 (Purchase Returns).
Net amount we owe = ₹45,000

Sharma & Co.: We bought ₹28,000 (Purchase Book) but returned ₹2,500 (Purchase Returns).
Net amount we owe = ₹25,500

4. Sales Returns Book (Returns Inward)

Remember: Goods returned BY customers who bought from us on credit.

Sales Returns Book of ABC Traders:

Date Particulars Credit Note No. L.F. Amount (₹)
Jan 15, 2025 Kumar Enterprises CN-01 12 3,000
Total 3,000

Ledger Posting:

Sales Returns A/c Dr. ₹3,000

To Sundry Debtors A/c ₹3,000

(Being goods returned by customers)

Individual Posting:

• Kumar Enterprises A/c credited with ₹3,000

See the Connection:

Kumar Enterprises: They bought ₹35,000 (Sales Book) but returned ₹3,000 (Sales Returns).
Net amount they owe us = ₹32,000

Remember: For transactions that DON'T fit in other books.

Purchase Book - Complete Example

Transactions for January 2025:

Jan 5: Purchased goods from Raj Traders worth ₹25,000 on credit (Invoice No. 101)

Jan 10: Bought merchandise from Kumar & Co. for ₹18,500 on credit (Invoice No. 205)

Jan 15: Purchased stock from Sharma Enterprises ₹32,000 on credit (Invoice No. 310)

Jan 22: Acquired goods from Gupta Brothers ₹15,750 on credit (Invoice No. 425)

Jan 28: Purchased inventory from Modern Traders ₹21,200 on credit (Invoice No. 530)

Purchase Book Format:

Date Particulars Invoice No. L.F. Amount (₹)
Jan 5, 2025 Raj Traders 101 15 25,000
Jan 10, 2025 Kumar & Co. 205 22 18,500
Jan 15, 2025 Sharma Enterprises 310 28 32,000
Jan 22, 2025 Gupta Brothers 425 35 15,750
Jan 28, 2025 Modern Traders 530 42 21,200
Total Purchases for January 1,12,450

Ledger Posting:

Purchases A/c Dr. ₹1,12,450

To Sundry Creditors A/c ₹1,12,450

(Being goods purchased on credit during January)

Important: Individual creditor accounts are also posted separately with their respective amounts.
