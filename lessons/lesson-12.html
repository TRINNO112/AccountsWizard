Introduction to Books of Original Entry

Books of Original Entry are the first books where transactions are recorded chronologically before being posted to the ledger. These are also called Prime Entry Books or Subsidiary Books. They help organize transactions by type, making the accounting process more efficient and reducing the ledger's size.

Key Point: Subsidiary books record only CREDIT transactions (except Cash Book and Journal Proper). Cash transactions are recorded in the Cash Book.

Types of Subsidiary Books

Definition: A book used to record all credit purchases of goods intended for resale.

What is recorded:

  • Only credit purchases of trading goods
  • NOT recorded: Cash purchases, purchase of assets (furniture, machinery), purchase returns

Format:

Date Particulars (Supplier Name) Invoice No. L.F. Amount (₹)
01/01/25 ABC Traders INV-001 15 25,000
Total 25,000

Accounting Entry: Purchases A/c Dr. / To Sundry Creditors A/c

Definition: A book used to record all credit sales of goods.

What is recorded:

  • Only credit sales of trading goods
  • NOT recorded: Cash sales, sale of assets, sales returns

Format:

Date Particulars (Customer Name) Invoice No. L.F. Amount (₹)
05/01/25 XYZ Enterprises SALE-101 22 30,000
Total 30,000

Accounting Entry: Sundry Debtors A/c Dr. / To Sales A/c

Definition: A book used to record goods returned to suppliers from whom goods were purchased on credit.

What is recorded:

  • Goods returned to credit suppliers
  • Defective, damaged, or excess goods sent back
  • Debit Note is issued to the supplier

Format:

Date Particulars (Supplier Name) Debit Note No. L.F. Amount (₹)
10/01/25 ABC Traders DN-005 15 2,000
Total 2,000

Accounting Entry: Sundry Creditors A/c Dr. / To Purchase Returns A/c

Definition: A book used to record goods returned by customers who bought on credit.

What is recorded:

  • Goods returned by credit customers
  • Damaged, wrong, or defective goods received back
  • Credit Note is issued to the customer

Format:

Date Particulars (Customer Name) Credit Note No. L.F. Amount (₹)
12/01/25 XYZ Enterprises CN-008 22 1,500
Total 1,500

Accounting Entry: Sales Returns A/c Dr. / To Sundry Debtors A/c

Definition: A book used to record transactions that cannot be recorded in any other subsidiary book.

What is recorded:

  • Opening entries
  • Closing entries
  • Adjustment entries
  • Rectification of errors
  • Purchase/Sale of assets on credit
  • Writing off bad debts
  • Depreciation entries

Format:

Date Particulars L.F. Debit (₹) Credit (₹)
15/01/25 Furniture A/c Dr.
To ABC Suppliers
(Being furniture purchased on credit)		 8
18		 15,000
15,000
Note: Journal Proper is the ONLY subsidiary book that records both debit and credit aspects of a transaction.

Special
