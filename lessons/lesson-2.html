Introduction to Basic Accounting Terms

Understanding basic accounting terms is crucial. These terms form the foundation of accounting language. Without them, it’s difficult to interpret financial records or communicate in business.

Key Accounting Terms

An entity is any unit (person, organization, or business) that carries on economic activities independently.

The business entity concept states that the business is treated as separate from its owner(s) for accounting purposes.

An event is any happening that has an economic impact on the business. All events may not be transactions, but all transactions are events.

An account is a record that summarizes all transactions relating to a particular item such as assets, liabilities, income, or expenses.

Capital is the amount invested by the owner in the business for earning profit.

Drawings are amounts or goods withdrawn by the owner for personal use. They reduce owner’s equity.

Liabilities are obligations that a business owes to outsiders, e.g., creditors, loans, outstanding expenses.

Assets are resources owned by the business which provide future economic benefits. They are classified as:

  • Tangible Assets: Physical assets such as machinery, buildings, furniture.
  • Intangible Assets: Non-physical assets such as goodwill, patents, trademarks.
  • Current Assets: Short-term assets like cash, debtors, stock, expected to be used within one year.
  • Fictitious/Nominal Assets: Not real assets, but expenditure not written off (e.g., preliminary expenses).

Expenditure is the amount spent or liability incurred to acquire goods, services, or assets. It can be capital, revenue, or deferred expenditure.

Expenses are the cost incurred in earning revenue, e.g., rent, salary, electricity. They are deducted from revenue to calculate profit.

Key Definitions
GROSS PROFIT

Basic measure of trading results

  • Gross Profit = Sales – Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
  • Shows efficiency of core business
NET PROFIT

Final earnings after expenses

  • Net Profit = Gross Profit – Expenses
  • Represents true profitability
CASH SALES

Goods sold with instant payment

  • Buyer pays cash immediately
  • No outstanding balance
CREDIT SALES

Goods sold with delayed payment

  • Customer owes business
  • Recorded as Debtors
CASH PURCHASES

Goods bought with immediate payment

  • Business pays instantly
  • No liability created
CREDIT PURCHASES

Goods bought with deferred payment

  • Business owes supplier
  • Recorded as Creditors

Accounting Equation

ASSETS = CAPITAL + LIABILITIES
