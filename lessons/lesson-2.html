Introduction to Basic Accounting Terms

Understanding basic accounting terms is crucial for anyone studying accounting. These terms form the foundation of accounting language and help in understanding financial transactions and statements. In this lesson, we'll cover essential accounting terminology that every student should know.

Key Terms and Definitions

1. Transaction

A transaction is any event that involves the exchange of value between two or more entities. It can be:

  • Purchase of goods
  • Sale of services
  • Payment of expenses
  • Receipt of income

2. Account

An account is a systematic record of all transactions relating to a particular person, asset, liability, expense, or income. Types of accounts include:

  • Personal Accounts (accounts of persons or entities)
  • Real Accounts (accounts of assets)
  • Nominal Accounts (accounts of expenses and incomes)

3. Capital

Capital represents the amount invested by the owner in the business. It is also known as owner's equity. Features of capital:

  • Initial investment by owner
  • Can be increased through additional investments
  • Can be decreased through withdrawals

4. Asset

Assets are economic resources owned by a business that have value and can generate future economic benefits. Examples include:

  • Cash and Bank Balance
  • Inventory
  • Machinery and Equipment
  • Buildings

5. Liability

Liabilities are amounts owed by the business to external parties. Common types include:

  • Bank Loans
  • Accounts Payable
  • Outstanding Expenses
